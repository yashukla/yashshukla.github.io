Mckay Airway

A novel oral airway device that keeps patients breathing in critical care situations.

  • Affiliation: Soteria Medtech
  • Website: soteriamedtech.ca

The Mckay Airway is a proprietary medical device that simplifies airway management. It reduces the technical skills and training required to keep a patient's airway open. The device is effective with both unconcious and awake patients. The Mckay Airway falls under Class I Health Canada classification, and has successfully been used in trial procedures.

TACET (Total Aural Communication Experience Translator)

A haptic feedback vest for deaf users to experience music by feeling it through their skin.

  • Client: SaskInvent
  • Website: saskinvent.com

TACET aims to be a device that allows members of the deaf community to experience music through vibrations felt on the user's skin. There are over 350,000 deaf individuals in Canada, and over 53 million around the globe. Members of the deaf community are limited in the number of ways that they can communicate with and interact with the world i.e. mainly through American Sign Language (ASL), speech, and written text. Music, for the most part, has not been fully communicated through these modes. As such, music is mostly a foreign concept for those deaf from birth, and is a source of frustration for those who have experienced it before. This will be achieved by converting audio signals (music) into physical vibrations that can be felt through the body’s skin receptors.

Ben'fit

An app that promotes physical fitness, individually tailored for each user by culture and gender.

  • Affiliation: MADMUC Lab
  • Website: madmuc.usask.ca

Ben'Fit is an app meant to encourage users to compelete regular physical activity. What make Ben'Fit different from other physical fitness apps is that the user experience is individually tailored for each user. The app changes the activies presented to the user based on the user's gender and nationality. Research has shown that this tailoring significantly improves the amount of physical activity a user completes, compared to apps that do not tailor user experiences.
